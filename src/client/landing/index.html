Where are your memories?

The urge to store memories and share stories is ancient. The oldest stored memories that we know off are 32,000 years old cave paintings depicting the animals that our ancestors would see daily. We believe that we can collectively do better than that in storing our own memories and eventually letting our descendants know of our values, thoughts and feelings.

There is so much about us in what we casually write on our social networks, store on our cloud drives and send privately over email. We spread our digital memorabilia with the comings and goings of fads but all our memories still have the time and the place, its context and the depth to it.

Memory Dive aims to fill the void between ourselves and the silos of personal and public data that we all generate. We have created it as a safe place to mix all our digital data, give it context and be able to freely search over it.

What do we do?

Gather

We gather the data from the services you choose to connect and from it create for you your own memory diving place.

Search

We give you text search of all your data and of things you referenced in it, like web pages.

Remember

We send you memory of the day email every day so you don't have to dive down the memory well all by yourself.

Analyze

We give you analytics and insights into your own data, public and personal.

Backup

We give you a one-click complete backup of all your data we have gathered.

There is zero wiggle room with your privacy

We never share your data

Earning and keeping your trust is our #1 concern - and there is only one way to approach it: we will never share your data with anyone.

We do not keep around any of your data

We do not keep your data beyond the usefulness of our service to you, our client. If you were to leave our service, we would irreversibly delete all of your data.

We never peek

We don't read, analyze, identify or otherwise do anything with your data beyond providing our service to you. Nor do our hardware, software, service providers, delegates and so on.

The three exceptions

We gather irreversibly anonymous service usage data which we analyze in order to improve.

We may be compelled by law to share your data with government and law-enforcement agencies.

We may be compelled by law to keep your data beyond the termination of the service.
